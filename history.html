Then and Now

A short history of Lakeville

Lakeville's story starts with roads, farms, lakes, and small-town growth before becoming one of the larger cities in the south metro area.

1853

Dodd Road begins

Captain William B. Dodd helped design a road through the area to connect military forts in Minnesota Territory. The road still gives Lakeville a connection to its early transportation history.

1855

Early settlement

J. J. Brackett platted land near Prairie Lake, which later became known as Lake Marion. The area grew as a farming community with people moving in from several European backgrounds.

1910

Dan Patch era

Marion Savage built the Dan Patch railroad line to reach Antlers Amusement Park. This made the lake and the community better known around the region.

Today

A growing city

Lakeville is now a fast-growing suburb with schools, businesses, neighborhoods, parks, and access to the wider Twin Cities metro area.
